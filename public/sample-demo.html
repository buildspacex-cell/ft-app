Chapter 1 · Business Story READ ONCE

What Data Patterns actually does

Data Patterns does not make the tank. It does not make the missile. It works on the electronics that help defence and space systems see, think, talk, and respond.

The body analogy

If a defence system were a human body, the metal platform is the body. The radar is the eyes. The communication system is the voice. The processors and electronics are the brain and nerves. Data Patterns works in that brain-and-nerves layer.

Defence needRadar, satellite, aircraft, missile, EW
→
Data PatternsDesigns high-reliability electronics
→
Mission systemCan sense, process, guide, or communicate

What they sell

Think of the custom electronic modules, processing boards, radar sub-systems, and space-grade electronics that sit inside larger defence and aerospace programs.

Why trust matters

In a phone, a glitch is annoying. In a radar, satellite, or aircraft system, a glitch can be mission failure. That is why qualification, engineering depth, and past execution matter more than advertising.

Why revenue can be lumpy

This is not like selling soap every day. It is more like building parts for a space mission or a railway signalling system: orders arrive in batches, testing takes time, and delivery depends on program schedules.

Chapter 3 · Trust Check

Can the story be trusted?

The story has to leave proof. For a company like Data Patterns, the proof is simple: promised work should become delivered work, delivered work should leave money behind, and money should eventually come in.

The footprint analogy

A headline tells you what happened outside the house. The accounts show the footprints inside. If work is growing but money is not coming in, something is dragging. If the company says it is doing high-value work but keeps less money from each rupee, the work may be getting harder. If materials pile up, it may be preparing for delivery - or carrying risk.

Promises vs delivery

Promised work is not the same as delivered work. The question is whether what customers promised is actually turning into completed work.

Chapter 5 · Price Sense

Is the story already too expensive?

A great company can still be a hard purchase if the price already assumes everything goes right. The question is not just “is the story good?” It is “how much of the good story is already in the price?”

The house analogy

A great house in a great neighborhood can still be a bad deal if you pay too much for it. The house may be wonderful. The problem is the price. Stocks work the same way: business quality and buying price are two different questions.

Price today

The story looks expensive, not impossible.

The market is already giving credit for defence localization, complex electronics, and future order growth. That does not make the company bad. It means the future has to arrive cleanly.
